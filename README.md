# 📌 Abstract
[](https://github.com/Aishu1312/Fake-job-analyzer/tree/main#-abstract)
The increasing popularity of online job portals has created new opportunities for job seekers, but it has also led to a rise in fraudulent job postings. Fake Job Analyzer is an Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning-based system developed to identify and analyze suspicious job advertisements. The project utilizes Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques and machine learning algorithms to examine job descriptions and predict whether a job posting is genuine or potentially fraudulent.

The system extracts important textual features from job advertisements, processes them through a trained machine learning model, and generates a prediction along with a risk assessment score. By automating fraud detection, the platform helps job seekers avoid scams, financial losses, and identity theft. The project is designed to provide a user-friendly interface where users can submit job descriptions and instantly receive authenticity analysis. The results demonstrate the effectiveness of machine learning in detecting fraudulent recruitment activities and improving online job security.

# 📖 Introduction
[](https://github.com/Aishu1312/Fake-job-analyzer/tree/main#-introduction)
With the rapid growth of online recruitment platforms, millions of job opportunities are posted every day. While these platforms provide convenience and accessibility, they also attract scammers who create fake job advertisements to exploit job seekers. These fraudulent postings often promise unrealistic salaries, demand registration fees, or collect personal information under false pretenses.

Fake Job Analyzer was developed to address this growing issue by leveraging Artificial Intelligence and Natural Language Processing techniques to identify suspicious job postings automatically. The system analyzes textual patterns and characteristics commonly associated with fraudulent advertisements and classifies them as genuine or fake.

The primary objective of this project is to assist job seekers in making informed decisions and reducing the risk of falling victim to employment scams. By providing real-time job authenticity analysis, the system contributes to a safer and more trustworthy online recruitment ecosystem.

# ⚙️ Methodology
[](https://github.com/Aishu1312/Fake-job-analyzer/tree/main#%EF%B8%8F-methodology)
The Fake Job Analyzer follows a machine learning pipeline for fraud detection. The user provides a job description through the application interface. The input text undergoes preprocessing, including cleaning, tokenization, and feature extraction. NLP techniques such as TF-IDF vectorization transform textual data into numerical representations suitable for machine learning models.

The processed data is then passed to a trained classification model that predicts whether the job posting is genuine or fraudulent. Additionally, a risk score is calculated based on detected suspicious indicators. The final result is displayed to the user along with recommendations and risk assessment information.

## ⚙️ How It Works

1. **Text Preprocessing** — The job description is lowercased and cleaned using regex.
2. **TF-IDF Vectorization** — Text is converted into numerical feature vectors.
3. **ML Classification** — A trained Logistic Regression model predicts Real or Fake.
4. **Risk Scoring** — A rule-based engine scans for 25 known scam keywords and calculates a risk score.
5. **Results** — The app displays the verdict, confidence probability, risk score, and suspicious indicators found.
